RBI Monetary Policy Committee — First Bi-Monthly Review, FY 2026-27
Meeting date: April 2026

DECISION
The MPC unanimously kept the policy repo rate unchanged at 5.25%. As a
consequence, the Standing Deposit Facility (SDF) rate remained at 5.00%,
and both the Marginal Standing Facility (MSF) rate and the Bank Rate
remained at 5.50%. The Committee retained its "neutral" policy stance,
which it characterized as preserving flexibility to respond in either
direction as conditions evolve.

GROWTH OUTLOOK
The RBI projected FY27 real GDP growth at approximately 6.9%, revised
down modestly from earlier expectations. The Committee flagged elevated
uncertainty in the global environment as a factor tempering the outlook,
while noting that domestic high-frequency indicators had generally held
up.

INFLATION OUTLOOK
The RBI raised its FY27 CPI inflation projection to 4.6% (up from prior
estimates), citing persistently elevated energy prices connected to the
West Asia conflict, and possible El Niño-related weather risk, as upside
risks to the inflation outlook. The Governor noted that these energy and
weather-related pressures posed asymmetric risk to the upside rather
than the downside.

POLICY COMMUNICATION
The Committee's language emphasized close monitoring of both domestic
and global developments, rather than committing to a fixed rate path.
Markets read this as consistent with a "wait and watch" posture, holding
rates steady while reserving the option to act if inflation or currency
pressures intensified.

RISKS FLAGGED
- Elevated and volatile crude oil prices linked to Middle East conflict
- Possible below-normal monsoon / El Niño conditions affecting food
  prices
- General elevated uncertainty in the global macro environment
