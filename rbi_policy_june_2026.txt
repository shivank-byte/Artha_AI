RBI Monetary Policy Committee — Second Bi-Monthly Review, FY 2026-27
Meeting date: June 5, 2026
Governor: Sanjay Malhotra

DECISION
The MPC unanimously kept the policy repo rate unchanged at 5.25%, for a
second consecutive review. The SDF rate remained at 5.00%; the MSF rate
and Bank Rate remained at 5.50%. The Committee retained its "neutral"
policy stance.

GROWTH OUTLOOK — REVISED DOWN
The RBI lowered its FY27 GDP growth forecast to 6.6%, down from the 6.9%
projected in the April review. The Governor noted that despite this
downward revision, the Indian economy entered this period of global
turbulence with materially better fundamentals than in comparable past
episodes. High-frequency indicators suggested growth had held up
reasonably well since the escalation of the West Asia conflict, with
both private consumption and investment demand showing resilience.
Strong services momentum, continuing effects of GST rationalization, and
stable employment conditions were cited as supportive factors going
forward.

INFLATION OUTLOOK — REVISED UP
The RBI raised its FY27 CPI inflation forecast to 5.1%, up from 4.6% in
the April review. Core inflation (excluding food and fuel) was also
revised up, to 4.7% from 4.4%. The Committee attributed the recent
uptick in headline inflation mainly to food inflation, noting that core
inflation remained comparatively benign — and that core inflation
excluding precious metals was notably lower still, offering some
comfort. However, the Committee flagged that higher energy and other
input costs had begun showing up in prices, visible in the recent WPI
(Wholesale Price Index) print. Going forward, retail inflation was
expected to rise further due to partial pass-through of energy prices to
domestic fuel prices, rising input costs, and the prospect of a
below-normal monsoon.

KEY RISKS FLAGGED
- Duration and intensity of the West Asia conflict — described as posing
  significant risk to both the growth and inflation baseline projections
- Prospects of a below-normal monsoon, separate from the conflict-driven
  risks
- Partial energy price pass-through still working its way into domestic
  prices

MEASURES TO SUPPORT THE RUPEE / ATTRACT CAPITAL
Alongside the rate decision, the RBI announced a set of measures aimed
at attracting foreign capital and easing pressure on the rupee and the
balance of payments:
- Expanded the Fully Accessible Route (FAR) to include all new 15-, 30-,
  and 40-year government securities
- Removed investment and concentration limits for Foreign Portfolio
  Investors (FPIs) under the General Route
- Increased equity investment limits for NRIs and OCIs, and extended
  similar treatment to all individual Persons Resident Outside India
- Introduced concessional forex swap arrangements (through Sept 2026) to
  incentivize External Commercial Borrowings by Public Sector
  Undertakings
- Committed to bearing full hedging cost (through Sept 2026) for banks
  raising fresh 3-5 year FCNR(B) deposits
- Restored the allowed time for realization of export proceeds to nine
  months, down from the temporarily extended fifteen months

MARKET REACTION NOTE
Ahead of the announcement, a section of the market had expected the RBI
might raise rates to defend the currency; the decision to hold was
described as a positive surprise, and fixed income markets rallied
across the curve following the announcement.
